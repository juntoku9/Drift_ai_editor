ATLAS TRAVEL COPILOT
Product Requirements Document
Owner: Maya Ortiz, Head of Product
Status: Working draft

Product Goal
Atlas converts freeform trip intent into complete day plans that users can act on immediately. Inputs include destination, dates, budget, constraints, and preference signals.

Launch Plan in Current Draft
Public beta date is March 20.
Initial market coverage includes United States, Japan, Italy, and Thailand.
Launch narrative assumes broad availability and complete plan-to-action flow.

Scope in Current Draft
One-prompt itinerary generation.
Hotel and activity recommendations.
Flight and hotel booking links.
Editable day-by-day planner.
Shareable itinerary links.

Performance and Reliability Expectations
p95 generation target under 10 seconds.
Confidence cues should be visible but lightweight.
Supplier freshness checks should run before rendering recommendations.

Success Metrics
Itinerary completion rate above 85 percent.
Booking-link click-through above 20 percent.
Day-14 retention above 24 percent.

Risks
Supplier reliability variability.
Stale pricing and availability mismatch.
User trust degradation if output quality drops during peak demand.

Open Questions
How much detail should first response include versus speed.
Whether multi-city itinerary is required in beta scope.
Whether confidence labels should be mandatory in all plan sections.
